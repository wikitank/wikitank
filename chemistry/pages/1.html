History and theories

Matter is anything that has mass and volume (occupies space).

Thales (600 BCE): "water is protosubstance - it's a first form of ice, liquid and vapour" (Protosubstance is the term that describes a hypothetical, undifferentiated state of matter or energy from which all forms of matter and energy arise).
"Matter: something which occupies a volume" (it was defined as theory in those time).

Empedocles (400 BCE): "air occupies a volume and must thus be matter."
Matter is composed of "the four elements: earth, air, fire and water."
Empedocles was not alone: a similar line of thought could be found in many different cultures

Democritos (contemporary with Empedocles) thought otherwise: he proposed that matter was composed of atoms.
Platon rejected this idea: if air was composed of small particles, they would fall to the ground like dust!
Platon and, particularly, Aristoteles made sure that the theory of ”The Four Elements” persisted until the 19th century!
Up till this day it is common that some very successful scientists become ”authorities” with a large impact.

Boyle (17th century) introduced something similar to Democritos’ idea that everything is composed of atoms:"Air consists of small particles (”corpuscles”) and the higher the pressure, the smaller the distance between the particles"
Dalton (1766-1844) discovered that gases and other substances react with each other in fixed proportions: "if you let tin ($Sn$) burn in the presence of oxygen ($O_2$) two different compounds are formed:

  • One of them contains $88.1\%$ w/w $Sn$ and $11.9\%$ w/w $O$
  • The other contains $78.7\%$ w/w $Sn$ och $21.3\%$ w/w $O$
$$Sn+O_2\rightarrow SnO+SnO_2$$
