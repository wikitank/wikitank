;### Prerequisite ;*.[lml] ;- Anthropocentric view ;* ;- Humans are important and pivotal, they are indespensible as a part of nature, and for nature. Nature is in the service of humans and humans needs. ;** ;- Biocentric (Zoocentric) view ;* ;- We are on of millions of species. But nature is not in the service of human needs, some other species might be more important for nature than humans, to keep nature running. ;** ;- Econentric view ;* ;- It's hard to speak about species in isolation from environment. Species are living organisms, but ecosystems include non-living factors such as mountain, river, water, etc. ;- There's disctinction between relevant and important. Relevant means important in context. ;- Symbiosis, many species are interconnected, humans and bacteria, water and plants, etc. ;** ;** ; The needs of certain species can conflict with the needs and intrests of other and some species are protected by law. --- ;### Nature and Humans ; Humans consider themselves special because they are able to speak through spoken symbolic language. s{If we humans are different, then why should we care about natur, and how should we care?
